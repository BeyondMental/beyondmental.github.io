Retainer Reformation

3D First Person Stealth Horror Game

Software Used

As the sole developer on this project, I utilized the Unreal Engine 5 alongside Quixel Bridge and 3ds Max for asset creation.

About Retainer Reformation

Retainer Reformation is a first person stealth game with horror and platforming elements. Waking up in your prison cell, you musy explore the abandoned facility. Hide from the enemies patrolling, solve puzzles, and find notes slowly uncovering the story of what is happening.

My Contributions

Unreal Prototyping

Unreal's rapid prototyping and level whiteboxing tools were designed to create and playtest all of the game's level's from scratch. After completing a whitebox, levels were expanded and developed using a combination of Quixel and original game assets. The end result was a game with quality level design and visuals.

Enemy Chase AI

Enemies throughout the game chase the player, with their AI being programmed using Unreal Engine's blueprint system. Enemies will wonder around, until they see a player within their vision cone. From there, they will chase the player until they either catch said player or lose site of them. Every enemy in the game also has animations associated with their AI states.

Puzzle Scripting

All the puzzles located throughout the game were programmed using the logic from Unreal Engine's blueprint system. While some of these puzzles are as simple as a lock and key, others require multiple simultaneous elements. These often require cutscenes would moving parts or scripts that control environmental hazards.
